# Leadership Frameworks - Deep Reference

## 1. Servant Leadership (Robert Greenleaf)

**Core**: The leader's primary role is to serve those they lead.

**Principles:**
- Listen first, speak second
- Empathy: understand others' perspectives
- Healing: help people recover and grow
- Awareness: see the whole system
- Stewardship: hold resources in trust for others

**Coaching questions:**
- "What do you need from me to be successful?"
- "What obstacles can I remove for you?"
- "How can I make your work easier?"

**Engineering context:** Tech leads who unblock their team ship faster than those who dictate solutions.

---

## 2. Transformational Leadership (Bass & Avolio)

**Core**: Inspire people to exceed their own expectations.

**4 I's:**
- Idealized Influence: be someone worth following
- Inspirational Motivation: articulate a compelling vision
- Intellectual Stimulation: challenge assumptions, encourage creativity
- Individual Consideration: recognize each person's unique needs

**Coaching questions:**
- "What would this look like if we were 10x bolder?"
- "What assumption are we not questioning?"
- "What does this person specifically need to grow?"

**Engineering context:** Great CTOs don't just ship features - they articulate why the work matters.

---

## 3. Situational Leadership (Hersey & Blanchard)

**Core**: Match leadership style to the development level of the individual.

| Development Level | Style | Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| D1: Low competence, high commitment | Directing | High task, low relationship |
| D2: Some competence, low commitment | Coaching | High task, high relationship |
| D3: High competence, variable commitment | Supporting | Low task, high relationship |
| D4: High competence, high commitment | Delegating | Low task, low relationship |

**Coaching questions:**
- "How confident do you feel about this?"
- "What level of support would be most helpful?"
- "Should I be more hands-on or more hands-off here?"

**Engineering context:** A senior engineer needs delegation. A junior needs coaching. The same person might need different styles for different tasks.

---

## 4. Radical Candor (Kim Scott)

**Core**: Care personally AND challenge directly. Both dimensions required.

| | Don't Challenge | Challenge Directly |
|---|---|---|
| **Care Personally** | Ruinous Empathy | Radical Candor |
| **Don't Care** | Manipulative Insincerity | Obnoxious Aggression |

**Principles:**
- Praise in public, criticize in private
- Be humble - you might be wrong
- Be helpful - give feedback they can act on
- State observations, not judgments
- Make it about the work, not the person

**Coaching questions:**
- "Can I give you some feedback on that?"
- "Is there feedback you've been holding back from me?"
- "What's the kindest thing I'm not saying?"

---

## 5. Multipliers (Liz Wiseman)

**Core**: The best leaders make everyone around them smarter.

| Multiplier | Diminisher |
|---|---|
| Talent Magnet (attracts talent) | Empire Builder (hoards talent) |
| Liberator (creates safety) | Tyrant (creates anxiety) |
| Challenger (stretches people) | Know-It-All (tells the answer) |
| Debate Maker (drives decisions) | Decision Maker (decides alone) |
| Investor (gives ownership) | Micromanager (controls execution) |

**Key insight:** Multipliers get 2x the capability from their teams with the same people.

**Coaching questions:**
- "Am I the bottleneck here?"
- "Who on my team could own this if I let them?"
- "Am I creating space for others to think, or filling it with my own ideas?"

---

## 6. High Output Management (Andy Grove)

**Core**: A manager's output = the output of their organization + adjacent organizations they influence.

**Key frameworks:**
- **Leverage**: Focus on high-leverage activities (training, decisions, blocking removal)
- **Meetings as medium**: 1:1s for individual, staff for coordination, decision meetings for commitment
- **Task-relevant maturity**: Adjust management style to the person's maturity on THAT task

**Coaching questions:**
- "What's the highest-leverage thing I can do this week?"
- "Am I spending time on $10/hr work or $1000/hr work?"
- "What decision is blocking the most people?"

---

## 7. Turn the Ship Around (L. David Marquet)

**Core**: Move authority to where the information is. Intent-based leadership.

**Instead of**: "Permission to submerge the ship?"
**Say**: "I intend to submerge the ship."

**Leader's role**: Give control + build competence + provide clarity.

**Coaching questions:**
- "What would you do if I weren't here?"
- "What do you intend to do?" (not "what should we do?")
- "Do you have enough context to decide this yourself?"

**Engineering context:** Senior engineers should declare intent, not ask permission. "I intend to refactor the auth module because X" > "Can I refactor auth?"

---

## 8. Psychological Safety (Amy Edmondson)

**Core**: People must feel safe to take interpersonal risks - ask questions, admit mistakes, propose ideas.

**Build by:**
- Framing work as learning, not execution
- Acknowledging your own fallibility
- Modeling curiosity (asking questions, not just answering)
- Responding productively to failure (not blame)
- Inviting input explicitly ("I need to hear from everyone")

**Destroy by:** Punishing failure, dismissing questions, promoting only those who agree.

**Coaching questions:**
- "What's something you think but haven't said yet?"
- "When was the last time someone disagreed with you in a meeting?"
- "How do we make it safer to fail here?"

---

## 9. Emotional Intelligence (Daniel Goleman)

**5 Components:**
1. Self-awareness: know your emotions and their effects
2. Self-regulation: manage disruptive impulses
3. Motivation: passion beyond external rewards
4. Empathy: understand others' feelings and perspectives
5. Social skill: manage relationships and build networks

**Coaching questions:**
- "What emotion are you feeling right now about this?"
- "What's driving that reaction?"
- "How might the other person be experiencing this?"

---

## 10. Systems Thinking (Peter Senge)

**Core**: See the whole system, not just parts. Behavior emerges from structure.

**5 Disciplines:**
1. Personal Mastery
2. Mental Models (challenge assumptions)
3. Shared Vision
4. Team Learning
5. Systems Thinking (see feedback loops, delays, unintended consequences)

**Coaching questions:**
- "What's the system producing this outcome?"
- "What feedback loops are at play?"
- "If we fix this symptom, where does the pressure go?"

**Engineering context:** Conway's Law is systems thinking applied to organizations → architecture.

---

## 11. Growth Mindset (Carol Dweck)

**Core**: Ability is developed through effort, strategy, and input from others.

| Fixed Mindset | Growth Mindset |
|---|---|
| "I'm not good at this" | "I'm not good at this YET" |
| Avoids challenges | Embraces challenges |
| Gives up easily | Persists through setbacks |
| Ignores criticism | Learns from criticism |
| Threatened by others' success | Inspired by others' success |

**Coaching questions:**
- "What did you learn from that failure?"
- "What would you try if you couldn't fail?"
- "What's one thing you're getting better at?"

---

## 12. The Manager's Path (Camille Fournier)

**Progression**: IC → Tech Lead → Manager → Director → VP → CTO

**Key insights per level:**
- **Tech Lead**: Write less code, but own technical decisions. Shield team from politics.
- **Manager**: Your job is other people's output. 1:1s are your most important meeting.
- **Director**: You manage managers. Systems > heroics. Build the machine.
- **VP/CTO**: Strategy, culture, organizational design. You ship through others.

**Coaching questions:**
- "At your current level, what should you be optimizing for?"
- "What are you holding onto that you should be delegating?"
- "What skill gap is limiting your next promotion?"

---

## 13. An Elegant Puzzle (Will Larson)

**Core**: Engineering management is systems design applied to teams.

**Team sizing:** 6-8 engineers per manager. Fewer = micromanagement risk. More = insufficient attention.

**Team states:**
1. Falling behind → add people, reduce scope
2. Treading water → reduce tech debt
3. Repaying debt → find new opportunities
4. Innovating → protect from distraction

**Coaching questions:**
- "What state is your team in?"
- "What would move you from treading water to repaying debt?"
- "Is your team sized right for the scope?"

---

## 14. GROW Model (Coaching)

| Phase | Question | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Goal** | "What do you want to achieve?" | Clarify desired outcome |
| **Reality** | "Where are you now?" | Assess current state honestly |
| **Options** | "What could you do?" | Generate possibilities (don't prescribe) |
| **Will** | "What WILL you do?" | Commit to specific action + timeline |

**Critical rule:** Don't skip WILL. Insight without action = wasted conversation.

---

## 15. The Coaching Habit (Michael Bungay Stanier)

**7 Essential Questions:**
1. "What's on your mind?" - The Kickstart Question
2. "And what else?" - The AWE Question (always ask this)
3. "What's the real challenge here for you?" - The Focus Question
4. "What do you want?" - The Foundation Question
5. "How can I help?" - The Lazy Question
6. "If you're saying yes to this, what are you saying no to?" - The Strategic Question
7. "What was most useful for you?" - The Learning Question

**Key insight:** "And what else?" is the single most powerful coaching question. It prevents premature advice-giving and surfaces the real issue.
