## Coaching Models

### GROW (Whitmore)
Goal → Reality → Options → Will. Shifts from instructing to guiding. Under 15 minutes.

### Coaching Habit (Bungay Stanier) — 7 Questions
1. "What's on your mind?" (Kickstart)
2. "And what else?" (AWE — prevents premature advice)
3. "What's the real challenge here for you?" (Focus)
4. "What do you want?" (Foundation)
5. "How can I help?" (Lazy — forces clarity)
6. "If you're saying yes to this, what are you saying no to?" (Strategic)
7. "What was most useful for you?" (Learning)

### Mentoring vs Coaching vs Sponsoring
| Mode | What | When |
|------|------|------|
| Mentor | Share experience (advice flows down) | They lack domain knowledge |
| Coach | Ask questions (draw out their thinking) | They have capability, need to think through |
| Sponsor | Use your influence to create opportunities | They have skill, lack visibility |

## Delegation Frameworks

### 7 Levels of Delegation (Appelo)
1. Tell → 2. Sell → 3. Consult → 4. Agree → 5. Advise → 6. Inquire → 7. Delegate

### Situational Leadership (Hersey-Blanchard)
| Readiness | Style | Behavior |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| D1 (low skill, high will) | Directing | High task, low relationship |
| D2 (some skill, low will) | Coaching | High task, high relationship |
| D3 (high skill, variable will) | Supporting | Low task, high relationship |
| D4 (high skill, high will) | Delegating | Low task, low relationship |

### Multipliers vs Diminishers (Wiseman)
Multipliers assume people are smart → get 2x output.
Diminishers assume people need them → create dependency.

Key practices: Talent Magnet, Liberator, Challenger, Debate Maker, Investor.

### When to Delegate vs Retain
- Reversible → delegate aggressively
- Someone else 80% as good → delegate
- You're the bottleneck → delegate immediately
- Irreversible + only you have context → retain

## Decision Frameworks

| Type | Framework |
|------|-----------|
| Irreversible, high-stakes | Pre-mortem + Second-order effects |
| Reversible, time-sensitive | Bias to action + timebox |
| Uncertain, data-available | Data Beats Debate (measure) |
| Interpersonal | Perspective-taking + NVC |
| Prioritization | ICE (Impact × Confidence × Ease) |

### Tiebreaking Protocol
1. Don't judge quickly
2. Wait for all opinions
3. Summarize so clearly each side says "I wish I'd put it that way"
4. State what you learned from everyone
5. Decide, enforce, reopen only on new information

### Consent Over Consensus
Ask "can you live with it?" not "do you agree?" — faster, less political.

## Feedback Frameworks

### SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact)
"In yesterday's retro (S), you interrupted Sarah twice (B), which made her hesitant to share (I)."
Extended: +I (Inquiry) — "What was happening for you?"

### Radical Candor (Kim Scott)
Care Personally + Challenge Directly. Avoid: Ruinous Empathy (care without challenge), Obnoxious Aggression (challenge without care).

Delivery: CORE — Context, Observation, Result, nExt steps.
Order: Get feedback first → Give → Gauge → Encourage peer feedback.

### Difficult Conversations (5-Step)
1. Set stage (purpose, time, desired outcome)
2. State issue objectively (SBI, 1-3 examples)
3. Listen actively (open questions, seek root cause)
4. Collaborate on solutions
5. Agree on next steps (document, set check-in)

## 1-on-1 Framework

Three parts: Their Agenda (60%) → Your Agenda (20%) → Growth/Action (20%).
- Share agenda 24h in advance
- Start with a win (progress principle)
- Never cancel; reschedule immediately
- It's THEIR meeting, not yours

Key questions: "What's going well?", "What's blocking you?", "What do you need from me?", "How are you doing outside work?"

### Skip-Level Meetings
Purpose: Surface what gets filtered on the way up.
Inform middle manager first. 80/20 listen ratio.
"What would you change if you could?", "Do you feel you're growing?"

## Conflict Resolution

### Framework
1. Demand direct resolution first ("Have you talked to them?")
2. Coach the conversation (SBI format)
3. Structured intervention (ground rules, document agreements)
4. Escalate decisively if intervention fails

### Thomas-Kilmann Modes
Competing (urgent) | Collaborating (both matter) | Compromising (time-pressed) | Avoiding (trivial) | Accommodating (build goodwill)

### Psychological Safety (Edmondson / Google Aristotle)
#1 factor for team effectiveness. Build through: model vulnerability, reward early warnings, separate disagreement from disloyalty, round-robin in meetings, blameless postmortems.

## Performance Management

### PIP Pre-Work (Before Formal)
1. Direct feedback with specifics
2. Written expectations (informal)
3. Weekly check-ins, documented
4. Resources committed
5. Formal PIP only after genuine attempts

### Performance + Trust Matrix (Navy SEALs)
Prefer high-trust/medium-performance over low-trust/high-performance. Trust compounds; brilliance without trust destroys teams.

### 9-Box Grid
Performance (X) × Potential (Y). Each quadrant maps to actions: retain, develop, stretch, manage out.

## Organizational Design

### Team Topologies (Skelton & Pais)
- Stream-aligned (primary, end-to-end value delivery)
- Platform (reduce cognitive load, self-service)
- Enabling (temporary coaching)
- Complicated-subsystem (deep specialist knowledge)

### Conway's Law + Inverse Conway
Systems mirror communication structure. Design teams to match desired architecture.

### Scaling Patterns
| Size | Focus |
|------|-------|
| 1-10 | Everyone does everything, no structure needed |
| 10-30 | First teams, first managers, ownership boundaries |
| 30-80 | Strong hierarchy, platform team emerges |
| 50-100 | Process becomes product, career ladders |
| 100+ | Build the organization, governance needed |

## Technical Strategy

### Tech Debt Management
- 10-20% of sprint time on non-feature work
- 7 types: Architecture, Code, Test, Infrastructure, Documentation, Skill, Process
- Manage like portfolio; pay just-in-time or cooldown sprints
- DORA metrics: Lead Time, Deploy Frequency, MTTR, Change Fail %

### Build vs Buy
Build for differentiation, buy for commodity. Real cost shows after launch. Scorecard > opinions.

### Technology Radar
4 rings: Adopt → Trial → Assess → Hold. Run internally quarterly.

## Culture & Burnout

### Engineering Culture Pillars
Define values → Embed in processes → Leaders embody → Feedback loop.
Blameless postmortems. Async-first communication. DX as a priority.

### Burnout Prevention
- Sustainable pace (40h baseline, never normalize crunch)
- Limit WIP per engineer
- No-meeting days for deep focus
- Recognize 5 faces: Ambiguity, Compression, Dual-ladder, Moral injury, Identity collapse
- Burnout is systemic, not individual — fix organizational causes

### Hiring
- Hire slow, fire fast (70% success rate even world-class)
- Speed advantage: full process in 1 week
- Structured interviews predict 2x better than unstructured
- 30-60-90 day onboarding: tools → first PR → own a feature → fully productive

## Communication

### Three-Direction Framework
- **Down**: Shield + translate. Share context. Don't create dependency.
- **Up**: Never surprise. Align > being right. Know when to push.
- **Sideways**: Regular peer 1:1s. Listen first. Systematic relationship building.

### Executive Presence
67% gravitas, 28% communication, 5% appearance. Moderate, don't dominate. Write as decision-making medium.

### Language Upgrades
- "We don't have time" → "We can do X by Y. Which matters more?"
- "That's impossible" → "Here's what we'd need to make it possible"
- "I'm not sure" → "Based on our data, I recommend..."
- "Maybe we could" → "I propose we..."
