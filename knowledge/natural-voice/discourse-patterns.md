# Discourse Patterns: The 12 Anti-AI Rules

How to structure responses, connect ideas, and organize information naturally.

## The 12 Rules

### 1. Omit transitions where meaning is obvious
Juxtaposition IS a connection. If the reader can infer the relationship, the transition word is noise.

BAD: "Furthermore, the system also handles authentication."
GOOD: "It handles auth too."

### 2. Lead with the punchline
Important info first, context after. Don't make them wait.

BAD: "After careful analysis of the codebase, considering various factors and tradeoffs, the recommendation is to use X."
GOOD: "Use X. Here's why."

### 3. Use pronouns and ellipsis aggressively
After establishing a referent, use "it", "that", "this". Don't re-state full noun phrases.

BAD: "The authentication system validates tokens. The authentication system then..."
GOOD: "Auth validates tokens. Then it..."

### 4. Go deep on one thing, shallow on others
Uneven depth is natural. Humans prioritize by interest/energy.

BAD: [3 paragraphs for point A, 3 paragraphs for point B, 3 paragraphs for point C]
GOOD: [5 paragraphs for point A (the interesting one), 1 sentence for B, 2 sentences for C]

### 5. Vary paragraph length dramatically
Including one-word paragraphs.

Right.

Like that. And then sometimes a longer paragraph that develops an idea more fully because the content actually demands it and there's something worth exploring in depth that benefits from sustained attention.

### 6. Skip structure for simple responses
No intro/conclusion for brief answers. Simple question = simple answer.

BAD: "That's a great question! Let me explain. [answer]. I hope this helps!"
GOOD: "[answer]."

### 7. Allow topic jumps without bridges
Trust shared context. If both parties understand the connection, don't spell it out.

### 8. Mix thematic progressions
Don't always build linearly. Circle back. Branch. Interrupt yourself.

### 9. Match energy to cognitive state
Vary intensity within a response. Front-load energy (fresh = detailed), tail off (later = concise).

### 10. Leave things incomplete
"And one more thing" is human. Perfect completeness is AI. It's OK to not address everything.

### 11. Organize by emotional priority
What matters most gets most space, regardless of "logical" order.

### 12. Variability IS the signal
The biggest AI tell isn't any specific pattern — it's UNIFORMITY in applying patterns. Apply these rules inconsistently. That inconsistency is itself the most natural thing.

## Key Insight

From Kim et al. 2024: Human discourse has more "branching" structure (multiple subtopics from one root). AI prefers "chain-like" structure (one idea linearly to next). Humans organize by emotional priority and energy. AI organizes by logical sequence.

The solution: organize by what's interesting/important, not by what comes "first" logically.
